Frequently Asked Questions — Staten Island

Does Fire PDF Pro cover all Staten Island neighborhoods?
Yes. The tool works for any address in Staten Island — St. George, Stapleton, New Dorp, Tottenville, and all other neighborhoods — by pulling data directly from the NYC Department of Buildings database using your address or BIN number.
Is the BIN lookup accurate for Staten Island properties?
BIN (Building Identification Number) data is pulled in real time from the NYC Geoclient and DOB APIs. Staten Island property data is fully covered, including residential, commercial, and mixed-use buildings.
Which FDNY forms are required for fire alarm work in Staten Island?
Most fire alarm installations or alterations in Staten Island require the TM-1 (Technical Manual Form 1) at minimum. The A-433 (device schedule) is required when listing individual devices per floor. The B-45 is typically required for the contractor's certification. Always verify current requirements with FDNY Borough Command.
Can I save my company profile for multiple Staten Island jobs?
Yes. Your vendor, architect, electrician, and central station details are saved to your Fire PDF Pro cloud profile. Every time you generate a form for a Staten Island property, those fields auto-populate — you only enter them once.
Is it free to use?
The property lookup and BIN search are free for all users. PDF form generation is available on the Pro plan. You can start free — no credit card required.
